From identification strategy to interpretable results.

Each codethrough is structured around a real policy question — a seat belt law, a workers' comp reform, a union wage premium — and works forward from the causal identification problem to fully annotated, reproducible R code.

The goal isn't to memorize estimators. It's to know when a design is credible, when it isn't, and how to communicate results to a policy audience that doesn't read regression tables for fun.

Design Principle 01

Identification Before Estimation

Every module opens with the threat to causal inference — autocorrelation, heterogeneity bias, ability bias, sorting at a cutoff — before introducing the method that resolves it.

Design Principle 02

Real Data, Real Ambiguity

Results don't always confirm the hypothesis. The seat belt law, the union wage premium, the incumbency advantage — all are contested. That's the point.

Design Principle 03

Code You Can Adapt

Annotated line by line. Every function explained, every assumption tested. Fork the code, swap in your own data, and take the method into practice.
